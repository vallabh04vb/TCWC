About TCWC

The Clean Waters Challenge

An initiative by Bharat Clean Rivers Foundation to engage communities and raise awareness about plastic pollution in our water systems. Mumbai's top students will unite to creatively express their thoughts through art and creative writing.

Our Purpose

Through Fine Art, Literary Art, and Essay Writing students will express their vision for a cleaner future, turning creativity into awareness and action. The Clean Waters Challenge aims to harness the creative potential of young minds to address one of the most pressing environmental issues of our time.

Our Aim

To instil a deep sense of environmental responsibility in young minds, with a vision to scale the movement across India in the coming years. By engaging students in creative expression focused on water conservation and plastic pollution, we hope to create a generation of environmentally conscious citizens who will lead the charge in protecting our water bodies.

Why This Matters

Water pollution, particularly plastic pollution, is one of the most significant environmental challenges facing India today. With 47 of the 1,000 most polluted rivers globally located in Maharashtra alone, there is an urgent need for awareness and action. The Clean Waters Challenge seeks to address this issue by engaging young people in creative problem-solving and advocacy.

Our Impact

Community Engagement

Bringing together students, educators, and environmental advocates to work toward a common goal.

Awareness Creation

Using art and writing to spread awareness about water pollution and conservation.

Action Inspiration

Motivating young people to take concrete steps toward environmental protection.

Policy Influence

Creating a movement that can influence environmental policy and corporate practices.

Competition Categories

Fine Art

Express your vision for clean waters through painting, drawing, or sculpture.

  • Open to students aged 12-22
  • Artwork must be original
  • Theme: "A Blue Future"

Literary Art

Create poetry, short stories, or plays that highlight water conservation.

  • Open to students aged 12-22
  • Word limit: 1,500 words
  • Can be in English, Hindi, or Marathi

Essay Writing

Research and write about solutions to plastic pollution in our waters.

  • Open to students aged 15-22
  • Word limit: 2,000 words
  • Must include research and citations
